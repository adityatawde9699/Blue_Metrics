Why Monitor Water Quality?

Monitoring water quality is essential to ensure the safety and health of ecosystems and human populations. It helps in detecting pollutants, managing water resources, and ensuring compliance with environmental regulations.

Parameters We Measure

  • pH Level: Indicates the acidity or alkalinity of water, which is crucial for aquatic life.
  • Turbidity: Measures the clarity of water, affecting light penetration and aquatic plants.
  • Total Dissolved Solids (TDS): Indicates the concentration of dissolved substances in water, impacting water quality.
  • Conductivity: Measures the water's ability to conduct electricity, correlating with the concentration of ions and overall water purity.
  • Dissolved Oxygen (DO): Indicates the amount of oxygen available in water for aquatic life, essential for their survival.
